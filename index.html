Additional Notes

Route of administration · cross-checking these figures
Route of administration (IV vs. oral)
Every entry in this calculator is an IV/parenteral formulation, and this dataset does not include matching oral products, so no per-drug switch calculation is shown. For context: a published NHS analysis found IV ciprofloxacin's carbon footprint to be about 60× that of the oral tablet, and a separate 2024 study estimated a 20% reduction in unnecessary antibiotic courses could save roughly 4,200 tonnes CO₂e per month across NHS England. IV-to-oral switching is a standard stewardship lever — add oral-formulation CO₂e/waste factors to DATA to enable a real per-drug comparison here.
Cross-checking these emission factors
NHS England's public Medicine Carbon Footprint (MCF) Formulary, built from the peer-reviewed MCF Classifier methodology, independently rates thousands of medicines (low/medium/high/very high). It's a useful place to sanity-check this workbook's CO₂e figures for a given drug — this tool does not import or duplicate its per-product ratings.
About the manufacturing discharge figure above
Source: AMR Industry Alliance Predicted No-Effect Concentration (PNEC) table (rev. 2026). This models manufacturing-effluent dilution science — not what actually happens to clinical waste after hospital disposal — so treat it as an order-of-magnitude illustration, not a site-specific risk assessment. Antifungals/antivirals are outside this antibacterial-only framework and are excluded.
Emission factors and per-agent, per-dose, and per-product CO₂e and waste data are drawn from the source Antimicrobial Emissions Calculator workbook. Greenhouse gas equivalency conversions (miles driven, gallons of gas, pounds of coal, phones charged) are based on U.S. EPA methodology: epa.gov/energy/greenhouse-gases-equivalencies-calculator. This tool is intended to support antimicrobial stewardship and sustainability education and does not constitute clinical guidance.

Manufacturing discharge / resistance-selection-risk figures use the AMR Industry Alliance's published Predicted No-Effect Concentration (PNEC) table (rev. 2026), amrindustryalliance.org. This is not re-derived or independently re-verified beyond the public source cited in the code comments — treat it as indicative, not as a substitute for institutional environmental reporting.
